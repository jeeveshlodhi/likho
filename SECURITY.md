# Security Policy

Likho takes security seriously. We appreciate the efforts of security researchers and the broader community in helping us keep Likho and its users safe. This document outlines our security policy and how to responsibly disclose vulnerabilities.

---

## Supported Versions

Only the following versions of Likho currently receive security updates:

| Version | Supported          |
| ------- | ------------------ |
| 0.1.x   | :white_check_mark: Yes |
| < 0.1   | :x: No             |

If you are using an unsupported version, please upgrade to the latest release to receive security fixes.

---

## Reporting a Vulnerability

**Please do NOT open a public GitHub issue to report a security vulnerability.** Public disclosure before a fix is available puts all Likho users at risk.

Instead, please report vulnerabilities via email:

**:envelope: security@likho.app**

### What to Include in Your Report

To help us triage and resolve the issue as quickly as possible, please include the following in your report:

1. **Description** — A clear and concise description of the vulnerability, including the type of issue (e.g. SQL injection, authentication bypass, data exposure, etc.).
2. **Steps to Reproduce** — A detailed, step-by-step guide to reproducing the vulnerability reliably.
3. **Impact Assessment** — Your assessment of the potential impact, including which data, systems, or users could be affected.
4. **Proof of Concept** — A proof-of-concept (PoC) exploit, screenshot, or video demonstrating the issue. Even a partial PoC is helpful.
5. **Environment** — The version of Likho, operating system, and any other relevant context.

The more detail you provide, the faster we can validate and address the issue.

---

## Response Timeline

We are committed to handling security reports promptly:

| Stage             | Target Timeline                          |
| ----------------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Acknowledgement   | Within **48 hours** of receiving report  |
| Triage & Assessment | Within **7 days** of acknowledgement   |
| Fix & Patch Release | Dependent on severity and complexity   |
| Public Disclosure | Coordinated with reporter after patching |

We will keep you informed throughout the process with regular updates on our progress.

---

## Our Commitments to Reporters

When you report a vulnerability to us in good faith, we commit to the following:

- **Timely communication** — We will acknowledge your report promptly and keep you updated as we work toward resolution.
- **Credit** — With your permission, we will acknowledge your contribution in the release notes and/or a security advisory when the fix is published. Just let us know your preferred name or handle (or opt out entirely — your choice).
- **No legal action** — We will not pursue legal action against researchers who discover and report vulnerabilities responsibly and in accordance with this policy. We consider good-faith security research to be a valuable contribution to the community.
- **Confidentiality** — We will not share your personal information with third parties without your consent, except as required by law.

## Scope

### In Scope

The following are considered in scope for security reports:

- **Likho desktop application** (macOS, Windows, Linux) — client-side code, local data storage, and IPC mechanisms
- **Backend API** — REST/WebSocket endpoints, server-side logic, and data handling
- **Authentication system** — login flows, session management, token handling, and access controls
- **Data encryption** — encryption at rest and in transit, key management, and secure storage of sensitive notes and user data
- **Real-time collaboration layer** — Yjs-based sync, conflict resolution, and permission enforcement
- **Sharing and workspace features** — access control, permission scoping, and link-sharing mechanisms

### Out of Scope

The following are generally **not** in scope:

- **Third-party dependencies** — Vulnerabilities in upstream libraries (e.g. Electron, llama.cpp, BlockNote) should be reported directly to their respective maintainers. We still appreciate a heads-up if you believe a dependency puts Likho users at specific risk.
- **Social engineering** — Attacks that require manipulating Likho team members or users through deception (phishing, pretexting, etc.)
- **Physical attacks** — Vulnerabilities requiring physical access to a user's device
- **Denial-of-service** — Volumetric DoS/DDoS attacks against our infrastructure
- **Issues in unsupported versions** — Security issues that only affect versions older than `0.1.x`
- **Reports generated by automated scanners** without manual validation or demonstrated exploitability

If you are unsure whether an issue falls within scope, please reach out anyway — we would rather review a borderline report than miss a real vulnerability.

## Safe Harbor

Likho supports responsible security research. We consider security research conducted under this policy to constitute "authorized" activity under the Computer Fraud and Abuse Act (CFAA), the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (DMCA), and equivalent laws in other jurisdictions.

Provided that you:

- Act in good faith and make every effort to avoid privacy violations, data destruction, or degradation of service
- Only interact with accounts and data you own or have explicit permission to test
- Do not exfiltrate, alter, or destroy data
- Cease testing and report immediately upon discovery of any user data
- Do not perform testing that could harm the availability or integrity of Likho's systems or data

...we will not initiate or recommend legal action against you in connection with your research. If legal action is initiated by a third party, we will make clear that your actions were conducted under this policy and in good faith.

## PGP Encryption

If you would like to encrypt your security report for added confidentiality, a PGP public key can be provided upon request. Please send an unencrypted email to **security@likho.app** asking for our PGP key, and we will reply with the key details promptly.
